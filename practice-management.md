### Common Healthcare Policies

#### No-Show Policy
```markdown
# Patient No-Show and Late Cancellation Policy

**Policy Number:** PAT-001
**Effective Date:** [Date]

## Purpose
To establish guidelines for managing patient no-shows and late cancellations to ensure efficient scheduling and access to care for all patients.

## Policy

### Definition of No-Show
A no-show occurs when a patient fails to arrive for a scheduled appointment without prior notification.

### Definition of Late Cancellation
A late cancellation occurs when a patient cancels an appointment with less than [24/48] hours' notice.

### First Occurrence
- Patient will be contacted and appointment rescheduled
- Educational reminder about policy provided
- Documented in patient record

### Second Occurrence (within 12 months)
- Written warning sent to patient
- Policy acknowledgment required
- Documented in patient record

### Third Occurrence (within 12 months)
- Review by practice manager
- Potential dismissal from practice
- Patient notified in writing

### Exceptions
Exceptions may be made at provider discretion for:
- Medical emergencies
- Severe weather events
- Death in immediate family
- Other documented extenuating circumstances

### Fees
[Choose one:]
☐ No fee charged
☐ $[Amount] fee for no-show (not billable to insurance)
☐ Fee waived for first occurrence

### Patient Communication
- Policy provided to all new patients
- Reminder calls/texts include cancellation instructions
- Policy posted in waiting area and on website

## Staff Procedures

1. Document all no-shows in scheduling system
2. Attempt to contact patient within 24 hours
3. Send written communication after second occurrence
4. Escalate to manager after third occurrence
5. Maintain documentation for all actions taken
```

#### Social Media Policy
```markdown
# Social Media Policy for Healthcare Staff

**Policy Number:** HR-015
**Effective Date:** [Date]

## Purpose
To provide guidelines for appropriate use of social media by practice employees while protecting patient privacy, the practice's reputation, and legal compliance.

## Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and volunteers of [Practice Name] regarding their use of social media platforms including but not limited to: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, blogs, and online forums.

## Policy

### Patient Privacy (HIPAA Compliance)
**NEVER:**
- Post any patient information (names, photos, conditions, treatments)
- Discuss specific patient cases, even without names
- Take photos or videos of patients or patient areas
- Share patient communications or messages
- "Friend" or connect with current patients on personal accounts

### Professional Representation
**DO:**
- Identify your opinions as your own, not the practice's
- Be respectful and professional in all posts
- Consider how posts reflect on the practice
- Report any HIPAA violations you observe

**DON'T:**
- Post content that could be seen as discriminatory
- Make negative comments about patients, coworkers, or competitors
- Share confidential business information
- Post during work hours (unless job-related and approved)
- Use practice logos or branding without approval

### Official Practice Accounts
- Only designated staff may post to official accounts
- All content must be approved by [designated person]
- Patient testimonials require written HIPAA authorization
- Marketing posts must comply with advertising regulations

### Personal Accounts
- You may not list [Practice Name] as employer without approval
- Personal posts should not reference work or patients
- Privacy settings are recommended but not sufficient protection
- The practice may monitor public posts

## Violations
Violations may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Termination
- Legal action (for HIPAA violations)

## Reporting Concerns
Report social media concerns to:
- Practice Manager: [Name]
- HIPAA Privacy Officer: [Name]
```
