## PLAYBOOK 2: CONSULTING SALES ENGINE

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: LAND YOUR FIRST $5K CONSULTING CLIENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Objective: Sign first consulting client at $5,000+
Expected Result: $5,000-$15,000 contract
Difficulty: Medium
Time Required: 2-4 weeks

POSITIONING (Day 1-2)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 1: Define Your Expertise
  Answer:
  ├── What specific problem do you solve?
  ├── Who specifically has this problem?
  ├── What unique perspective do you bring?
  └── What results can you promise?

  EXAMPLE:
  "I help tech startups (10-100 employees) implement AI features
  in their products without building ML teams. I bring 5 years of
  production AI experience and can deliver working features in
  2-4 weeks instead of 2-4 months."

□ STEP 2: Package Your Offer

  OFFER STRUCTURE:

  AI IMPLEMENTATION SPRINT ($5,000-$15,000)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Duration: 2 weeks

  What's Included:
  • Discovery session (2 hours)
  • Requirements documentation
  • Architecture design
  • Implementation of [specific deliverable]
  • Testing and QA
  • Documentation and handoff
  • 2 weeks post-delivery support

  Outcome: Working [feature/system] ready for production

  Investment: Starting at $5,000
  (Final price based on scope)

□ STEP 3: Create Proof Points
  Gather:
  ├── Past project examples (even personal ones)
  ├── Skills/expertise credentials
  ├── Any testimonials (even informal ones)
  └── Metrics from previous work


PIPELINE BUILDING (Day 3-7)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 4: Identify 50 Target Prospects
  Criteria:
  ├── Company: Tech startup, 10-200 employees
  ├── Stage: Series A-B, or profitable
  ├── Signal: Building AI features, hiring AI roles
  └── Contact: CTO, VP Engineering, Head of AI

  Sources:
  ├── LinkedIn Sales Navigator
  ├── AngelList/Wellfound
  ├── Crunchbase (recent funding)
  ├── Twitter (following AI topics)
  └── Personal network

□ STEP 5: Research Top 10 Prospects
  For each:
  ├── Company description and product
  ├── Recent news/announcements
  ├── AI-related job postings
  ├── Contact's background
  └── Potential pain points

□ STEP 6: Write Personalized Outreach

  EMAIL TEMPLATE:
  "
  Subject: Quick idea for [Company]'s [AI initiative]

  Hi [Name],

  Noticed [Company] is [specific observation - hiring AI,
  building feature, etc.]. Exciting stuff.

  I've helped teams like [similar company] ship [type of
  feature] in weeks instead of months - most recently
  [brief case study].

  Would it be valuable to chat for 15 minutes about
  accelerating your AI roadmap?

  Either way, excited to see what [Company] ships next.

  [Signature]
  "


OUTREACH EXECUTION (Week 2)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 7: Launch Outreach Campaign
  Daily:
  ├── 10 personalized emails
  ├── 10 LinkedIn connection requests
  ├── 5 LinkedIn messages (to connections)
  └── 5 Twitter engagements

□ STEP 8: Follow-Up Sequence
  Day 0: Initial email
  Day 3: Follow-up email with value add
  Day 7: LinkedIn connection/message
  Day 14: Final email (close the loop)

□ STEP 9: Response Handling

  Positive Response:
  → Book discovery call immediately
  → Send calendar link
  → Confirm 24 hours before

  Objection Response:
  → Address specific concern
  → Offer alternative (smaller scope, etc.)
  → Keep door open for future


CLOSING (Week 3-4)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 10: Discovery Call Framework

  Structure (30-45 min):

  0-5 min: Rapport
  ├── Thank them for time
  ├── Quick intro about yourself
  └── Confirm time available

  5-20 min: Discovery
  ├── "Tell me about [AI initiative]"
  ├── "What's the biggest challenge?"
  ├── "What have you tried so far?"
  ├── "What does success look like?"
  └── "What's the timeline/urgency?"

  20-30 min: Present Solution
  ├── Summarize their situation
  ├── Present your approach
  ├── Share relevant examples
  └── Explain outcomes

  30-40 min: Close
  ├── "Does this approach make sense?"
  ├── "What questions do you have?"
  ├── "What would be the next step?"
  └── Propose timeline and investment

  40-45 min: Next Actions
  ├── Confirm next meeting
  ├── Agree on deliverables
  └── Set expectations

□ STEP 11: Proposal Creation

  PROPOSAL STRUCTURE:

  1. Executive Summary (1 paragraph)
  2. Understanding of Challenge
  3. Proposed Solution
  4. Deliverables (specific list)
  5. Timeline (weekly breakdown)
  6. Investment (with payment terms)
  7. About Us (brief bio + proof points)
  8. Next Steps (how to proceed)

□ STEP 12: Negotiation & Close

  Common Objections:

  "Too expensive"
  → "What budget did you have in mind?"
  → Offer phased approach or reduced scope

  "Need to think about it"
  → "What specifically are you considering?"
  → Address concerns directly
  → Create urgency: "My calendar is filling up"

  "Need approval from [other person]"
  → "Great, can I join a call with them?"
  → Ask: "What would help them say yes?"


EXPECTED RESULTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Week 1: 50 prospects identified, 20 outreach sent
Week 2: 5-10 responses, 3-5 discovery calls booked
Week 3: 2-3 proposals sent
Week 4: 1 deal closed ($5,000-$15,000)

Pipeline maintenance:
├── Ongoing outreach (20/week)
├── New prospects (10/week)
├── Continuous content for credibility
└── Referral requests from clients

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## PLAYBOOK 4: ENTERPRISE SALES MOTION

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: CLOSE A $100K+ ENTERPRISE DEAL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Objective: Close enterprise deal worth $100K+
Expected Result: $100K-$500K contract
Difficulty: Very High
Time Required: 3-6 months

TARGETING (Month 1)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 1: Define Enterprise ICP
  Company Characteristics:
  ├── Revenue: $50M+
  ├── Employees: 500+
  ├── Industry: [Target verticals]
  ├── Tech maturity: High
  └── AI budget: $500K+/year

  Buying Signals:
  ├── Hiring AI/ML roles (10+)
  ├── Recent AI announcements
  ├── Digital transformation initiative
  ├── Competitive pressure
  └── Executive mandate

□ STEP 2: Account Selection
  Identify 10-20 target accounts
  For each:
  ├── Company overview
  ├── AI/tech initiatives
  ├── Key stakeholders
  ├── Entry points
  └── Potential deal size

□ STEP 3: Stakeholder Mapping

  Typical Enterprise Buying Committee:
  ├── Economic Buyer: CFO, VP Finance
  ├── Technical Buyer: CTO, VP Engineering
  ├── User Buyer: Head of AI, ML Lead
  ├── Champion: Technical lead, innovator
  └── Blocker: Security, procurement

  For each stakeholder:
  ├── Name and role
  ├── LinkedIn profile
  ├── Priorities and concerns
  └── Communication style


ENGAGEMENT (Month 1-2)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 4: Multi-Thread Entry

  Path 1: Bottom-up (Champion)
  ├── Connect with technical leads
  ├── Provide value (content, tools)
  ├── Build internal advocate
  └── Get introduced upward

  Path 2: Top-down (Executive)
  ├── Executive referral/intro
  ├── Industry event connection
  ├── Thought leadership engagement
  └── Direct executive outreach

  Path 3: Existing Relationship
  ├── Past colleagues now there
  ├── Mutual connections
  ├── Investor/advisor network
  └── Customer referrals

□ STEP 5: Value-Based Engagement

  Touch 1: Share relevant insight
  Touch 2: Invite to exclusive content
  Touch 3: Offer mini assessment
  Touch 4: Propose discovery call

  Email Example:
  "
  Subject: AI investment trends at [Industry] companies

  Hi [Name],

  I put together an analysis of how companies like [Company]
  are investing in AI - thought you'd find it relevant given
  [Company]'s [initiative].

  Key insight: [Specific finding relevant to them]

  Would you be open to a brief call to discuss how this
  applies to [Company]'s roadmap?

  [Signature]
  "


DISCOVERY & QUALIFICATION (Month 2-3)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 6: Executive Discovery

  Questions to ask:
  ├── "What are the top 3 priorities for [AI/tech] this year?"
  ├── "How is [Company] approaching AI differently than peers?"
  ├── "What would success look like in 12 months?"
  ├── "What's getting in the way of moving faster?"
  └── "Who else needs to be involved in this conversation?"

□ STEP 7: Technical Discovery

  Questions to ask:
  ├── "Walk me through your current AI architecture"
  ├── "What tools/platforms are you using today?"
  ├── "Where are the biggest bottlenecks?"
  ├── "What would your ideal solution look like?"
  └── "What's been tried before? What worked/didn't?"

□ STEP 8: MEDDIC Qualification

  Metrics: What measurable outcomes do they need?
  Economic Buyer: Who controls budget?
  Decision Criteria: How will they decide?
  Decision Process: What's the timeline/steps?
  Identify Pain: What's the cost of inaction?
  Champion: Who's your internal advocate?


SOLUTION & PROPOSAL (Month 3-4)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 9: Solution Architecture

  Create:
  ├── Technical architecture diagram
  ├── Implementation roadmap
  ├── Integration requirements
  ├── Success metrics definition
  └── Risk mitigation plan

□ STEP 10: Business Case Development

  ROI Model:
  ├── Current state costs
  ├── Future state benefits
  ├── Implementation investment
  ├── Payback period
  └── Net benefit over 3 years

  Example:
  "
  Current State:
  • 6 engineers × $150K = $900K/year on AI infrastructure
  • 12-month average time to ship AI features
  • 40% of AI projects don't reach production

  Future State with Murphbeck:
  • 2 engineers needed = $300K/year (67% reduction)
  • 3-month average time to ship (4x faster)
  • 90% production success rate

  Investment: $150K/year
  Annual Savings: $600K
  ROI: 300%
  Payback: 3 months
  "

□ STEP 11: Proposal Development

  Enterprise Proposal Structure:
  1. Executive Summary
  2. Situation Analysis
  3. Proposed Solution
  4. Implementation Plan
  5. Success Metrics
  6. Investment and Terms
  7. Risk Mitigation
  8. About Murphbeck
  9. References
  10. Next Steps


NEGOTIATION & CLOSE (Month 4-6)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ STEP 12: Stakeholder Alignment

  Before proposal presentation:
  ├── Pre-sell to champion
  ├── Address technical concerns
  ├── Prepare for objections
  └── Confirm decision criteria

□ STEP 13: Procurement Navigation

  Common Requirements:
  ├── Security questionnaire
  ├── SOC 2 documentation
  ├── Insurance certificates
  ├── References
  ├── Legal review
  └── InfoSec review

  Pro Tips:
  ├── Ask for templates upfront
  ├── Pre-complete common items
  ├── Offer reference calls
  └── Be responsive (24hr SLA)

□ STEP 14: Contract Negotiation

  Negotiation Levers:
  ├── Contract term (multi-year for discount)
  ├── Scope adjustments
  ├── Payment terms
  ├── SLA levels
  └── Success-based components

  Hold the line on:
  ├── Core pricing (15% max discount)
  ├── IP ownership
  ├── Limitation of liability
  └── Indemnification scope

□ STEP 15: Close & Celebrate

  Closing Actions:
  ├── Final stakeholder buy-in
  ├── Contract execution
  ├── Payment processing
  ├── Kickoff scheduling
  └── Win announcement
```
