## QUESTION CATEGORIES

### Behavioral Questions (STAR Method)
```
FORMULA: Situation → Task → Action → Result

COMMON QUESTIONS:
• Tell me about a time you faced a difficult challenge
• Describe a situation where you had to lead a team
• Give an example of a conflict you resolved
• Tell me about a failure and what you learned
• Describe your greatest professional achievement

ANSWER STRUCTURE:
S - "The situation was..." (15% of answer)
T - "I was responsible for..." (10% of answer)
A - "So I..." (60% of answer - focus here!)
R - "As a result..." (15% of answer - quantify!)
```

### Technical Questions
```
BY FIELD:
Software: Coding challenges, system design, debugging
Marketing: Campaign strategy, analytics, channel expertise
Finance: Valuation, modeling, market analysis
Operations: Process improvement, logistics, efficiency
Sales: Pipeline management, objection handling, closing

APPROACH:
1. Clarify the question
2. Think out loud
3. Show your reasoning
4. Acknowledge limitations
5. Ask follow-ups if needed
```

### Situational Questions
```
FORMAT: "What would you do if..."

COMMON TYPES:
• Deadline pressure scenarios
• Disagreement with manager
• Ethical dilemmas
• Resource constraints
• Priority conflicts

APPROACH:
1. Acknowledge complexity
2. State your principles
3. Walk through your approach
4. Consider trade-offs
5. Explain your decision
```

### Case Questions
```
FRAMEWORK:
1. Listen and clarify
2. Structure your approach
3. Analyze systematically
4. Synthesize findings
5. Make recommendation

COMMON TYPES:
• Market sizing
• Profitability analysis
• Market entry
• Growth strategy
• Operations improvement
```

## THE STAR METHOD DEEP DIVE

### Perfect STAR Answer Template
```
SITUATION (Set the scene - 2-3 sentences)
"In my role as [position] at [company], I was
working on [project/context]. We were facing
[challenge/opportunity]."

TASK (Your specific responsibility - 1-2 sentences)
"I was responsible for [specific duty]. The goal
was to [measurable objective]."

ACTION (What YOU did - 4-6 sentences, most detail)
"First, I [initial step].
Then, I [next action].
I also [additional action].
When [obstacle] arose, I [how you addressed it]."

RESULT (Outcome with metrics - 2-3 sentences)
"As a result, we achieved [quantified outcome].
This led to [broader impact]. I learned [insight]."
```

### STAR Story Bank Topics
```
Prepare stories for:
□ Leadership / taking initiative
□ Teamwork / collaboration
□ Problem-solving / analytical thinking
□ Conflict resolution
□ Overcoming failure / resilience
□ Exceeding expectations
□ Innovation / creativity
□ Handling pressure / deadlines
□ Persuasion / influence
□ Learning quickly / adaptability
```

## COMMON QUESTIONS BY TYPE

### "Tell Me About Yourself"
```
STRUCTURE (Present → Past → Future):
"Currently, I [current role/focus].
Previously, I [relevant background].
I'm excited about this role because [connection]."

TIPS:
• Keep to 60-90 seconds
• Focus on professional, not personal
• Tailor to the role
• End with why you're here
```

### "Why This Company?"
```
STRUCTURE:
"Three things excite me about [Company]:
1. [Mission/product]: [specific aspect]
2. [Culture/values]: [evidence you've researched]
3. [Opportunity]: [how you'd contribute]"

TIPS:
• Be specific, not generic
• Reference recent news/achievements
• Connect to your experience
• Show genuine enthusiasm
```

### "What's Your Weakness?"
```
STRUCTURE:
"One area I've been working on is [real weakness].
For example, [brief example].
I've addressed this by [specific action taken].
Now, [evidence of improvement]."

TIPS:
• Choose a real but not critical weakness
• Show self-awareness
• Demonstrate improvement
• Don't say "perfectionism" or "work too hard"
```

### "Why Should We Hire You?"
```
STRUCTURE:
"Based on what you've shared, you need someone who
can [key requirement 1] and [key requirement 2].
My experience in [relevant area] directly applies
because [specific example].
Additionally, I bring [unique value proposition]."

TIPS:
• Reference the job description
• Provide evidence, not just claims
• Differentiate yourself
• Show enthusiasm
```

### "Where Do You See Yourself in 5 Years?"
```
STRUCTURE:
"In five years, I hope to have [realistic goal
aligned with role].
I'd like to develop expertise in [relevant skill]
and potentially [reasonable progression].
This role is a great fit because [connection]."

TIPS:
• Show ambition but be realistic
• Align with company trajectory
• Don't mention competitor companies
• Focus on growth, not titles
```

## QUESTIONS TO ASK THE INTERVIEWER

### About the Role
```
• What does success look like in the first 90 days?
• What are the biggest challenges facing this role?
• How does this position contribute to company goals?
• What happened to the previous person in this role?
```

### About the Team
```
• How would you describe the team culture?
• How does this team collaborate with other departments?
• What's the team's biggest priority right now?
• How do team members typically develop and grow?
```

### About the Company
```
• What excites you most about the company's future?
• How has the company changed since you joined?
• What's one thing you wish more candidates knew?
• How does the company approach [relevant topic]?
```

### Closing Questions
```
• Is there anything about my background that concerns you?
• What are the next steps in the interview process?
• When can I expect to hear back?
```

## INTERVIEW DAY CHECKLIST

### Before
```
□ Review your resume and key stories
□ Research company news (last 30 days)
□ Prepare 3-5 questions to ask
□ Plan outfit (professional, comfortable)
□ Confirm logistics (time, location, format)
□ Get good sleep the night before
□ Eat a proper meal
□ Arrive 10-15 minutes early
```

### During
```
□ Smile and make eye contact
□ Listen fully before answering
□ Take a breath before responding
□ Use the interviewer's name
□ Ask clarifying questions if needed
□ Keep answers to 2-3 minutes max
□ Show enthusiasm genuinely
□ Take notes briefly
```

### After
```
□ Send thank-you email within 24 hours
□ Reference specific conversation points
□ Reiterate interest and fit
□ Address any concerns raised
□ Follow up if no response in stated timeline
```
